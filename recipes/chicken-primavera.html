Chicken Primavera Pasta Bake

Description

This baked chicken primavera pasta features cavatappi, spring vegetables, and a creamy cheese sauce. Topped with breadcrumbs and Parmesan, it is baked until warm, bubbly, and lightly golden.

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces dried cavatappi pasta
  • 2 cups fresh or frozen peas
  • 1 pound asparagus, trimmed and coarsely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1 1/2 pounds skinless, boneless chicken breast cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 teaspoon salt, divided
  • 3/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, divided
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 (8 ounce) package cream cheese, softened
  • 3/4 cup reduced-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 cups coarsely chopped spinach
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese, divided
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh basil or 1 teaspoon dried basil, crushed
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh oregano, or 1 teaspoon dried oregano, crushed
  • 2/3 cup seasoned panko breadcrumbs

Steps:

Step 1:

Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Lightly grease a rectangular baking dish and set it aside.

Step 2:

Fill a 4- to 6-quart Dutch oven with salted water and bring it to a boil. Cook the pasta until just al dente, about 9 to 11 minutes. During the last 3 minutes, add the peas and asparagus to the pot. Drain the mixture, saving 2/3 cup of the pasta water. Leave the pasta and vegetables in the strainer, and keep the Dutch oven for the sauce.

Step 3:

While the pasta cooks, heat the oil in a 12-inch skillet over medium-high heat. Season the chicken with 1/2 teaspoon salt and 1/2 teaspoon black pepper, then add it to the hot skillet. Cook, stirring from time to time, until the chicken is browned and cooked through, about 5 to 7 minutes. The internal temperature should reach 165°F (74°C). Remove from the heat.

Step 4:

To make the sauce, melt the butter in the Dutch oven. Whisk in the flour, the remaining 1/2 teaspoon salt, and 1/4 teaspoon pepper. Add the milk, cream cheese, and chicken broth, whisking until smooth. Cook over medium heat, stirring often, until the sauce thickens and starts to bubble. Let it cook for 1 more minute, then whisk in the reserved pasta water, 2/3 cup Parmesan, basil, and oregano.

Step 5:

Return the pasta and vegetables to the Dutch oven and toss them in the sauce until well coated. Mix in the cooked chicken, spinach, and cherry tomatoes.

Step 6:

Transfer the mixture to the prepared baking dish. Top it with the remaining 1/3 cup Parmesan cheese and the panko breadcrumbs.

Step 7:

Bake for 15 to 20 minutes, or until the sauce is bubbling and the topping is crisp and golden brown.
